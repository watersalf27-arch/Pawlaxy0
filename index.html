Pet Knowledge Hub

1. The Scottish Fold: Ear Mutation

Scottish Folds are famous for their unique "folded" ears. This is a dominant genetic mutation affecting the cartilage throughout the body. Care Tip: Clean their ears weekly as the fold can trap wax easily.

2. The Sphynx: Hairless Mutation

The Sphynx is a breed known for its lack of a coat. This mutation is a natural genetic occurrence. Key Fact: Because they have no fur to absorb skin oils, Sphynx cats require regular bathing and protection from sunburn.

3. The Munchkin: Short-Leg Mutation

Munchkin cats are defined by very short legs caused by a genetic mutation called achondroplasia. Despite their legs, they are very active and can run and jump quite well. Health: They need a strict diet to avoid obesity, which can strain their spine.
